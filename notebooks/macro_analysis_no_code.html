Macro-Driven Cross-Asset Risk Analysis

Gold (GC=F) US Dollar Index (DXY) US 10Y Treasury Yield

Author: Yurou Chen
Date: February 2026

This report investigates cross-asset relationships between Gold, the US Dollar Index (DXY), and US 10Y Treasury yields using time-series analysis from 2020 to 2026.

The objective is to evaluate:

  • Correlation dynamics
  • Volatility regimes
  • Risk-adjusted performance
  • Drawdown characteristics
  • Macro-hedging properties

Executive Summary

Key findings:

Gold exhibits persistent negative correlation with DXY (~ -0.38).
60-day rolling correlation strengthened to approximately -0.45 in early 2026.
Gold displays higher short-term volatility (~3%) compared to DXY (~0.4%).
Gold delivered superior risk-adjusted returns (Sharpe 1.19).
US10Y experienced significantly deeper drawdowns (~ -73%).

These results confirm Golds macro-sensitive nature and partial hedge characteristics against USD strength.

Interpretation

Gold maintains a structurally negative relationship with the US Dollar Index.

During periods of intensified USD strength, gold prices tend to face downward pressure.

Rolling analysis reveals regime-dependent variation in correlation intensity, particularly visible in early 2026.

Despite higher volatility, gold delivered superior risk-adjusted returns, suggesting compensation for elevated risk exposure.

US10Y displayed significant drawdown risk, highlighting duration sensitivity during stress environments.

Volatility Regime Insight

Gold demonstrates different return behavior across volatility regimes.

High-volatility periods often coincide with macro stress environments, reinforcing golds defensive characteristics.

Conclusion

This cross-asset analysis confirms golds macro-driven sensitivity and partial hedge properties.

While exhibiting higher short-term volatility, gold delivered stronger risk-adjusted performance relative to DXY and US10Y.

These findings reinforce golds relevance in diversified macro portfolios.
